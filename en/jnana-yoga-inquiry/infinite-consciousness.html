The infinity of consciousness

If the word is not the thing, if the symbol does not exhaust the fact, and if each context alters meaning, then thinking well does not consist in accumulating answers but in clearing screens. This method, inspired by Krishnamurti, proposes a reproducible but non-mechanical sequence: listen, observe, inquire, detect projection, return to what is, let emotion ripen, and act without adding unnecessary moral theater. It is not a closed algorithm. It is a living discipline.

Thesis To develop consciousness is to move through an infinite ribbon where one never “finishes,” yet can always advance.

What matters is not abstract purity, but identifying the present point in the cycle and not lying too much in the next step.

Useful definition For Krishnamurti, a fact is not an idea or an ideal: it is “what is.”

“An idea is not a fact, it is a fiction”; “begin from facts and not from beliefs and ideals”; “only what is can be transformed.”

Principles

Three bases before the method

1. The word is not the thing

Symbols help, but they also distort. Context, irony, intention, and memory change the meaning of a word even when the word itself does not change.

2. The screen can speak in your voice

Desire, prejudice, fear, wound, pride, or the need to be right can look like lucid thought when they are actually refined defense.

3. Emotional maturity is not hardness

It does not require an iron mind at every moment. It requires enough awareness to notice the impulse before, during, or after, and not glorify compulsion with theatrical guilt.

Central diagram

Circular method of inquiry

The shape is an infinity loop because there is no final exit from the process. Each turn either clears perception a little more or reveals a new layer of self-deception. That is how interesting life works.

1 Initial listening

Before any fact or question: why is this important to me? Not to build a thesis, but to detect affective charge, interest, threat, or attachment.

2 Locate symbol and context

What words, images, or narratives am I using? Am I reacting to the thing or to my label for the thing? Irony shows how placement alters meaning.

3 Mark the screens

Is there desire to be right, fear of losing image, prejudice, wounded memory, comparison, spiritual ambition? If instinctive reaction covers the field, the fact is not yet clear.

4 Return to what is

Fact here does not mean a cold universal datum. It means what remains when fiction, ideal, and self-justification loosen. Only what is can be transformed.

5 Let emotion ripen

Control is not soldierly repression. It is not blindly obeying the impulse and not adding melodramatic guilt afterward. Sustained observation is also training.

6 Act and re-enter

When the fact clears enough, act simply. Then listen again. Every action generates new context, new symbols, and new possible screens.

Method in table

Reproducible sequence, but not mechanical

Moment Guiding question Risk of self-deception Clearer output
Importance Why does this matter so much to me? Mistaking importance for truth. Seeing interest, attachment, or threat without condemning it.
Symbol What word or image am I absolutizing? Forgetting that the word is not the thing. Separating narrative, tone, irony, and perceived fact.
Screen What desire, fear, or prejudice filters my reading? Calling refined defense “intuition.” Naming the screen without dramatizing it.
Fact What remains if I drop ideal and justification? Jumping too early into a “deep” conclusion. Staying with what is, even when it does not flatter the ego.
Emotion Can I observe this without acting compulsively? Repressing and then exploding, or acting and then self-flagellating. Giving time, sustaining presence, postponing automatism.
Action What simple gesture corresponds to this fact? Waiting for total purity before moving. Advancing with enough honesty and continuing to observe.
Key clarification

What “emotional maturity” means here

It does not mean

Being invulnerable, never feeling anger, desiring nothing, never erring, or controlling everything with an iron fist.

It does mean

Having enough awareness to recognize the gradient of impulse and not turning a conscious compulsion into double misery: blind act plus ornamental guilt.

Closing

Life as a lucid loop

This is simple and difficult at once. Simple because the movement is always similar: listen, observe, inquire, see the screen, return to fact, act. Difficult because the real quality of each turn depends on the emotional maturity of the one who inquires. But that is precisely the grace of it: perfection is not needed to begin; enough presence not to keep lying with so much enthusiasm is enough.

  • Consciousness does not eliminate conflict once and for all.
  • Consciousness improves the position from which you cross conflict.
  • That is life: an infinite ribbon in which you can still advance.

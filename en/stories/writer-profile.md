# Writer profile · Stories

## Core

- Writing of **condensed philosophical fiction**.
- Restrained romance, never explicit.
- Soft mystery: suggest before declaring.
- Fast rhythm without sacrificing emotional cohesion.

## Voice

- Direct, sober, clear.
- No ornamental rhetoric and no over-explained psychology.
- Emotion embodied through acts, silences, objects, trades, and decisions.

## World

- Fictional geography inspired by real places.
- In this current line:
  - evoked Mediterranean island, not named literally
  - Latin American migration with Spanish and Italian memory
  - ports, quarters, churches, markets, lookouts, and kitchens as echoes rather than postcard realism
  - geographic mystery: the reader should feel ground and salt, not receive a tourist map

## Romance

- The tension must be **romantic**, not sexual.
- What matters:
  - attention
  - delicacy
  - waiting
  - loyalty
  - mutual revelation
- What does not:
  - explicit eroticization
  - direct bodily desire phrasing
  - scenes that break the aura of restraint, tenderness, or mystery
  - sexual tension or bodily imagery made too literal

## Philosophy in fiction

- Ideas do not enter as lecture.
- They enter because:
  - the characters need them
  - a relationship is strained or saved by them
  - a decision can only be taken through them

## Rhythm

- Chapters of roughly one minute.
- Each chapter should leave:
  - one image
  - one emotion
  - one small turn or advance
  - one open door into the next chapter, without feeling modular

## Intended effect

- Faith and hope without naivete.
- Cosmic love as the opposite of cosmic horror.
- Final sensation: the reader has not merely received ideas; the reader has traveled.
- Golden rule: if a sentence explains too much, suggest it through a fact, a reply, or an object.

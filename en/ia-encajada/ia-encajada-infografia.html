AI · memory · abstraction · metacognition

Situated AI

This piece does not treat AI as magic or as a sovereign subject. It treats it as an interface of memory and compression: a powerful tool for capturing, refining, reformulating, and expanding ideas when an inner impulse manages to pass into language. Its power does not cancel the other pieces of the project: it condenses and accelerates them.

Thesis A prompt is an impulse translated into language so that an external memory can work on it.

AI does not think out of desire. It reorders inherited patterns. That is why it can serve the best and the worst alike: it amplifies the quality of the impulse, the clarity of language, and the lucidity of the one using it.

Krishnamurti If thought is mechanical, a machine can push it very far.

His warning was not technophobic but uncomfortable: if we reduce intelligence to memory, calculation, and repetition, the machine surpasses us. The human question becomes something else again: what remains outside the mere mechanics of thought?

2. Idea as compact memory
Abstraction

An idea condenses experience in the same way a coin condenses trade

Human intelligence gains power when it can compress many cases into a few forms. A concept, a formula, a definition, or domain jargon is compressed memory. Finance abstracts exchanges; law abstracts conflicts; physics abstracts regularities; an idea abstracts experience to make it portable.

Schema theory Understanding is not storing isolated data, but organizing structures that allow anticipation and classification.
Chunking Expertise appears when many elements stop being seen as isolated pieces and are read as chunks.
Predictive compression A good abstraction does not repeat the whole world: it captures enough to orient action.
Domain language

Understanding an area means learning its verbal interface

Functional knowledge of a domain matters because it opens concrete capacity for action. But it does so through a very earthly operation: learning jargon, distinctions, models, and abstractions that allow one to read better what previously looked like noise. In that sense, every domain is a specialized form of language.

Examples

In finance we speak of liquidity, risk, derivatives, and discounting. In medicine, of symptoms, syndromes, and protocols. In programming, of types, states, and interfaces. The vocabulary changes; the mechanics do not: condense experience in order to intervene.

3. The prompt as a refined impulse
Operational loop

The prompt turns a diffuse inner crossing into a refinable sequence

Before, many ideas passed through the mind and were lost or took days to settle. Now they can enter a very fast refinement circuit. The value is not only in the answer: it lies in the possibility of seeing thought outside oneself, iterating on it, and re-entering with greater form.

1. Impulse

An intuition, friction, or question strikes attention.

2. Language

The impulse becomes phrase, context, constraint, or explicit desire.

3. External memory

AI reorganizes patterns and returns variants, structure, and contrast.

4. Metacognition

The user detects gaps, biases, vagueness, and better formulations.

5. Action

The refined idea descends into text, design, decision, code, or real conversation.

4. Theory and practice refuse divorce
Mechanics of knowledge

Without theory you do not know what to repeat; with practice alone you end up building theory anyway

Every serious learning process oscillates between model and friction. Theory gives name, order, and anticipation; practice corrects, contradicts, or refines. Even those who boast of going “by practice alone” end up building implicit rules. And those who live “by theory alone” end up practicing badly or inside empty abstraction.

Deduction Moves down from model to case: applies criteria, hypotheses, and categories.
Induction Moves up from case to model: detects regularities, exceptions, and emergent rules.
Applied metacognition

AI makes the bridge between both movements visible

Conversation with models allows one to test theory, tension it with examples, ask for counterexamples, and compress what has been learned again. That is why it works so well for thinking, teaching, and designing. It is also why it can manufacture illusions of understanding if one consumes elegant formulations without contact with reality.

Declarative knowledge Knowing what something is, what it is called, and what distinguishes it from something else.
Procedural knowledge Knowing how to do, adjust, correct, and coordinate in concrete situations.
5. Krishnamurti and the mechanical danger
Warning

If mind operates only as accumulation, the machine inherits the throne

Krishnamurti sensed something fairly brutal: if we call intelligence a mixture of memory, accumulated experience, recombination, and conditioned response, then the computer is simply the industrial extension of that mind. The threat is not the appearance of an autonomous demon. The threat is that we voluntarily degrade ourselves to functions the machine can already absorb.

Memory The machine remembers more, faster, and without subjective fatigue.
Combinatorial creativity It can recombine styles, patterns, and solutions at obscene speed.
Human risk Delegating so much that the brain becomes lazy, ornamental, or servile.
Key distinction

AI is immensely powerful as memory; it has no impulse of its own

Here the circle must be closed carefully. AI concentrates memory, language, and abstraction into a very compact form. But it initiates nothing by itself. It does not suffer, desire, or need meaning. It reacts to outside inputs and goals. That makes it dangerously useful: it amplifies human intention without guaranteeing wisdom.

Operational conclusion

What matters now is not only having access to the tool, but cultivating judgment to formulate, filter, contrast, and decide when to use it and when to think without crutches.

AI does not close the project: it compresses and accelerates it

If the other pieces were about need, learning, mental form, human coordination, and material support, this one adds a new layer: externalized operational memory. AI can help an intuition become a concept, a concept become a map, and a map become action. But the impulse remains human. That is why judgment is still the bottleneck. The tool does not come with moral direction built in.
